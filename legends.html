Legends

These are some notable examples of creatures in East Asian folklore

A yuki-onna or 雪女 is a snow woman who is be dangerous to travelers. She is a dark-haired beauty with blue lips seeking to lure people to their death. One could chant certain prayers or carry hot water to throw in case of meeting such a dangerous ladies. Sometimes they would fall in love with an intended victim and live with them but eventually they would caught out and flee or even melt in a hot bath.
The zhenniao or 鴆鳥 is a bird living in the mountains of China with feathers so poisonous it could be used as a weapon or to create deadly yet unnoticeable poisons. Zhenniao were mentioned in many myths but also even encyclopedias and historical records. Although most modern day scholars believe the zhenniao never truly existed, recent discoveries of birds with poisonous feathers suggest there possibly could have been such a bird.
The haetae or 해태 had the body of a leopard with scales all over and horns on its head. It could eat fire, so it was considered protection against fire disasters and placed all over buildings to ward off fires. It also could make judgements on people, making it a symbol for official government buildings like the National Assembly.
